The Challenges We Face

Skills Gap

Women returning after a career break often find their skills are no longer up-to-date with current industry standards.

Lack of access to professional development and training opportunities can make it difficult to re-enter the workforce.

Bias & Stereotypes

Recruitment processes often hold unconscious biases against candidates with career gaps on their resumes.

These biases can lead to women being overlooked for roles they are perfectly qualified for, based on outdated perceptions.

Lack of Support

Many women lack the necessary support systems, such as mentorship and networking, to navigate their career return.

This isolation can hinder their job search and make it difficult to reintegrate into a professional environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about returning to work and using our platform

Focus on being honest while highlighting the skills and experiences you gained during your career break. Include volunteer work, freelancing, courses, or community involvement. Create a "Career Break" section that briefly explains the reason and emphasizes your continued professional development. Our free downloadable guide "Resume Reinvention" provides detailed strategies.

A returnship is a professional internship designed specifically for individuals returning to work after a career break. These programs typically last 3-6 months and provide training, mentorship, and hands-on experience to help participants update their skills and transition back to the workforce. Many returnships lead to permanent positions.

Consider your career goals and the industry you're targeting. Look at job descriptions in your field to identify required skills, then select courses that address any gaps. Our platform offers skills assessments to help identify areas for development. Start with one course and build from there. Remember, our course instructors are available in the forums to answer questions about specific offerings.

If you're just exploring options, start with our Semi-Free plan for basic access. The Weekly plan is ideal if you're actively job searching and need intensive support for a short period. The Monthly plan offers the best value for ongoing career development and networking. All paid plans include discounts on courses and seminars, with increasing benefits at each level.

Start by reconnecting with former colleagues and classmates. Join our community forums to meet others in similar situations. Attend virtual and in-person networking events in your industry. Update your LinkedIn profile and engage regularly on professional platforms. Consider informational interviews to learn about current industry trends. Our "Networking for Returners" guide provides step-by-step strategies tailored for women returning to work.
